<p><p>Quad-Flat-No-Lead (QFN) packaging is a popular choice in the semiconductor industry due to its compact design and efficient thermal performance. The market for QFN packages can be divided into two main types: punched type and sawn type. Punched type involves creating packages using a punch-and-die method, offering lower costs and high-volume production. In contrast, sawn type uses sawing techniques to achieve precise shapes, suitable for applications requiring intricate designs and higher-quality finishes, often used in specialized electronic devices.</p></p>
