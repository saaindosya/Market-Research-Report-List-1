Water cooling employs liquid circulation to transfer heat away from components, offering superior efficiency for high-performance systems. Thermoelectric cooling leverages solid-state devices to create a temperature gradient, providing precise temperature control, although it can be more complex and expensive. Each type serves specific application needs.</p></p>
