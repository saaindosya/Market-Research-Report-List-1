<p><p>The lacquer thinner market encompasses various types, primarily mineral spirits and oil of turpentine. Mineral spirits are petroleum-derived solvents commonly used for thinning paints and cleaning brushes, offering fast evaporation and low odor. Oil of turpentine, extracted from pine trees, serves as a natural solvent with a stronger scent and is often preferred for certain artistic applications. Both types cater to different preferences in terms of performance, environmental considerations, and application methods within the broader lacquer thinner market.</p></p>
