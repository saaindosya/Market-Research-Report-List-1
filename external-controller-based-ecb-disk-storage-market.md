<p><p>External Controller-based (ECB) disk storage encompasses two primary market types: Fiber Channel (FC) and Internet Small Computer System Interface (iSCSI). FC is a high-speed network technology designed for storage area networks, offering low-latency and high throughput for enterprise applications. In contrast, iSCSI uses standard Ethernet networks to facilitate storage access over IP, making it more cost-effective and simpler to manage for diverse environments. Both serve to enhance data storage performance and scalability, catering to varying organizational needs.</p></p>
