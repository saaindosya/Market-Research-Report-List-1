<p><p>The acid dyes market is categorized into strong acid dyes and weak acid dyes, each serving distinct applications. Strong acid dyes typically have high solubility and vivid color retention, making them suitable for dyeing protein fibers like silk and wool. They are often used in industrial applications. Weak acid dyes, on the other hand, have lower acidity and are generally more suitable for synthetic fibers and applications requiring less aggressive staining. Both types cater to diverse industries, influencing their market dynamics.</p></p>
