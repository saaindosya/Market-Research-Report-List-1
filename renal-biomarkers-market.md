<p><p>The renal biomarkers market is segmented into several types based on the methods used for detection and analysis. Enzyme Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) utilizes antibodies and color change to quantify biomarkers. Enzymatic Assays measure chemical reactions to indicate renal function. Turbidimetric Immunoassays assess the cloudiness produced when antigens and antibodies interact, providing sensitive quantification. Other methods may include mass spectrometry and molecular techniques, expanding the options for renal disease monitoring and diagnostics, catering to varied laboratory needs.</p></p>
