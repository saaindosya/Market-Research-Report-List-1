<p><p>Diffuse Large B Cell Lymphoma (DLBCL) treatment involves a combination of drugs such as Rituxan, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin, Vincristine, and Prednisone. Rituxan, a monoclonal antibody, targets CD20 on B cells. Cyclophosphamide is an alkylating agent that interferes with DNA replication. Doxorubicin is an anthracycline antibiotic that disrupts cancer cell growth. Vincristine, derived from the periwinkle plant, inhibits microtubule formation, while Prednisone, a corticosteroid, reduces inflammation and cancer growth. Together, these agents form a key regimen in DLBCL management.</p></p>
